Embeddings & Cosine Similarity

Embeddings turn meaning into vectors; cosine similarity measures whether two vectors point the same way. Drag the arrows, search by meaning, and do the famous king − man + woman ≈ queen analogy.

vectorscosinesemantic searchanalogies

1 · Cosine = direction, not length

Drag the tips of A and B. Cosine similarity is (A·B)/(|A||B|) — it depends only on the angle, so scaling a vector doesn't change it.

2 · Search by meaning

Each sentence is embedded (here a simple bag-of-words vector). Pick a query; results are ranked by cosine similarity — sentences that share meaning score high even when worded differently.

3 · Word arithmetic — the analogy trick

Embeddings place related words in related directions, so you can do algebra on meaning. We compute king − man + woman and find its nearest word.

The result vector (✦) lands nearest to queen — the model learned the "royal" and "gender" directions from data.

Why it matters: every modern retrieval system — search, RAG, recommendation — runs on embeddings + cosine similarity. See it power RAG and vector search.
